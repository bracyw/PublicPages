Confluence Consolidation & Change Recommendations

Software docs live in two homes on our Confluence: the shared car trees (NER25, NER27 …), where every team documents each build cycle and software’s current work is genuinely alive — and a top-level “Software” section, the team’s front door, which froze in 2023–24 and routes to none of it. This is a short proposal to bring the space in line with the 2026 Charter — what to archive, what to rename, what to create, and one convention so it stays clean. Nothing has been changed yet.

01

The problem, briefly

  • The car trees work — keep them. The space gives each car generation a shared tree (NER25, NER27) where every team files its cycle work. Mechanical’s and Electrical’s branches sit beside “Embedded Software” — the firmware branch, where the 27A cycle is being built right now — and FinishLine’s week-old “Web Development” branch. Software has filed its cycle docs this way since NER24.
  • The team’s front door froze. The top-level “Software” section — the evergreen home for onboarding, FAQ, learning resources, FinishLine, and Product — has 33 of its 39 pages untouched since 2024. The onboarding root names heads from two cycles ago, routes to an archived Slack channel, and offers paths for only two of the four subteams; the org page describes a team merge that was later abandoned; and nothing links the car trees. Browsing from “Software” reaches none of the live work.
  • The car branches still speak pre-Charter. “Embedded Software” is the old umbrella — its own 25 page defines it as “Firmware – Electrical Team + Applications Software – Software Team,” an org the Charter replaced. Application Software’s docs sit buried inside it, and FinishLine’s branch is titled “Web Development.” A newcomer can’t map the Charter’s four subteams onto the space.
  • Cycles supersede but never archive, and titles mislead. Embedded Software 24 sits unarchived beside 25, an empty Odyssey 24A beside a real 25A, and FinishLine’s old branch holds six roadmap cycles side by side. “Web Dev Onboarding” is FinishLine-only; two stale 2024 Launchpads sit in the Software section while the live one (Firmware Launchpad 26-27) lives under NER27; one page has sat in draft since Sep 2024.
The goal is a space that reads correctly when browsed. Years in titles are fine — the problem is the mix, and a front door that goes nowhere. Give each kind of page its home — evergreen in the Software section, cycle work under the car tree — name the branches after the org that exists, and archive superseded pages instead of leaving them as siblings.

The convention

Two kinds of page Cycle pages (onboarding flows, rosters, roadmaps, goals) always carry the year — <Subteam> <Topic> 26-27 — and live under the car tree, so newest wins. Evergreen pages (setup guides, charters, indexes) never carry a year; they live in the Software section with a “last verified” date in the body.
Titles say subteam + topic A title must be correct out of context, because search shows it out of context. No more “Web Dev Onboarding.”
One current page per topic Superseding a page means archiving its predecessor in the same edit. The car branches already year-stamp their cycles — archiving the old one at handoff is the only missing step; the Software section needs both habits.
Roles, not names Evergreen pages say “the Application Software head,” not a person — people rotate yearly and the roster names them.
Per-cycle ritual At head turnover: copy cycle pages to the new year, archive the old ones, re-verify evergreen pages. ~1 hour per subteam, once a year — this proposal never has to be written again.
