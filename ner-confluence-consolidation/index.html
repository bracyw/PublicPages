The problem, briefly

  • The live work is under the car trees. Software’s branches sit in NER27 beside Mechanical’s and Electrical’s folders — where every team plans annually.
  • The top-level “Software” section froze in 2024 — 33 of 39 pages stale, heads from two cycles ago, archived Slack channels — and routes to none of the live work.
  • The branch names are pre-Charter: “Embedded Software” (App Software buried inside) and “Web Development” (actually FinishLine).
  • Nothing archives: 24-cycle pages beside 25s, six FinishLine roadmaps side by side, four competing firmware onboarding pages — and no App Software onboarding at all.
The fix is to organize the space the way we already work: annual planning, around the car — one home per year, one folder that walks forward, archive the rest.

The six rules

Two kinds of page Cycle pages carry the year — <Subteam> <Topic> 26-27 — and archive with their tree. Shared Resources pages don’t, and move forward at handoff. The year-stamp is the archive bit.
Titles Subteam + topic, correct out of context. No more “Web Dev Onboarding.”
Two archive triggers Supersede → archive the predecessor in the same sitting. Car retires → its whole Software branch archives. Never delete — archived pages stay linkable.
Names Fine in cycle pages (they’re snapshots). Shared Resources says roles — those pages outlive rosters.
Durable links READMEs and pins link Shared Resources IDs only — they survive the walk. Cycle pages get a new ID every year.
The ritual At handoff: new branch, move Shared Resources in, write the year’s pages, archive the old. ~1 hour per subteam, per year.
